Why Brass?

Brass instruments are some of the most powerful and expressive instruments in the world of music. From the bright sound of the trumpet to the deep voice of the tuba, brass instruments can create a wide range of emotions and colours.

What are brass instruments?

Brass instruments create sound when a player vibrates their lips against a mouthpiece. This vibration travels through the instrument's tubing, producing different pitches. Valves or slides are used to change the length of the tubing and control the notes played.

Why play a brass instrument?

Powerful Sound

Brass instruments can produce strong, energetic sounds that can fill large concert halls and outdoor spaces.

Expressive Playing

Brass players can create many different moods through changes in tone, dynamics, and articulation.

Musical Opportunities

Brass instruments are used in orchestras, jazz bands, concert bands, marching bands, and many other ensembles.

Brass Across Music

Brass instruments have played an important role throughout music history. They are used in classical works by composers such as Mozart and Mahler, as well as modern genres including jazz, pop, and film music.

Start Your Brass Journey

Whether you want to play melodies, create powerful harmonies, or be part of an ensemble, brass instruments offer a rewarding musical experience.
